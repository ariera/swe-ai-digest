AI cybersecurity is not proof of work (2026-04-16)

Antirez argues that AI-driven cybersecurity is not analogous to proof-of-work: simply throwing more GPU compute at vulnerability discovery has diminishing returns, because LLM sampling paths saturate and model intelligence becomes the binding constraint. Using the OpenBSD SACK bug as a case study, he demonstrates that weaker models hallucinate plausible-sounding bugs without genuine reasoning, while only sufficiently capable models can chain multiple subtle conditions into a real exploit. For security-focused engineers, this suggests that model quality — not raw inference scale — will determine offensive and defensive AI capability.
