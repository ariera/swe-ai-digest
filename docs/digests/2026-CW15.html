Google AI Edge Gallery (2026-04-06)

Eight years of wanting, three months of building with AI (2026-04-05)

Willison highlights Lalit Maganti's detailed account of building syntaqlite — a SQLite linting and parsing library — over three months using Claude Code after eight years of procrastination. The key finding: AI excels at low-level, tedious, objectively-checkable implementation work (e.g., working through 400+ grammar rules), enabling rapid prototyping. However, AI actively hindered architectural decision-making: cheap refactoring discouraged committing to designs, and the first AI-generated prototype had to be discarded entirely. Maganti concludes AI is "unhelpful to harmful" when the developer doesn't know what they want. For engineers adopting coding agents, the article provides a nuanced, evidence-based framework for where agent assistance adds versus destroys value.
