Collina applies an academic economic framework to AI's impact on software engineering, arguing that the "Red Queen Effect" (constant model obsolescence), the Jevons Paradox (cheaper AI creates more demand, not less), and the "Wrapper Trap" (thin app layers get displaced by improving foundation models) together make human judgment — not implementation skill — the scarce, durable resource.
